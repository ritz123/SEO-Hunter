============================================================
  COLD CALL SCRIPT — Stage 5 (Phone / Walk-in)
============================================================

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
PHONE CALL SCRIPT
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

PREPARATION (before dialling)
  • Pull up the business's Google Maps listing
  • Have their audit score and top 3 issues in front of you
  • Know one competitor in the same city who ranks above them
  • Know the business's review count and star rating

──────────────────────────────
OPENING (first 8 seconds)
──────────────────────────────

"Hi, is this [Owner First Name]? Great — my name is [Your Name],
I'm a local SEO consultant based in [Your City]. Do you have
90 seconds? I found something on your Google listing I thought
you'd want to know about."

  → If they say YES: continue to Discovery
  → If they say BUSY: "Totally understand. Can I send you a quick
    email with what I found? What's the best address?"
  → If they say NO or hang up: note and follow up by email/walk-in

──────────────────────────────
HOOK — lead with business impact
──────────────────────────────

"When I search for [service] in [City] on Google, [Competitor Name]
shows up in the top 3. [Business Name] doesn't appear at all on
the first page — even though you have [X] reviews and have clearly
been around a long time."

  Pause. Let them respond.

──────────────────────────────
DISCOVERY — ask, don't pitch
──────────────────────────────

"Out of curiosity — do you get many calls or bookings through
your website, or do most customers find you through referrals
and the Google listing?"

  → If referrals / GBP: "That's common for established businesses.
    The thing is, a lot of new customers default to Google search
    first — especially for urgent needs like [plumbing / dental
    emergencies / etc.]. If your site has technical issues, you're
    invisible to them."

  → If they say website is fine: "Totally — I thought the same,
    which is why I ran it through Google's own tool. There are
    [X] signals flagged. Happy to share the full report."

──────────────────────────────
ISSUE REVEAL (top 2-3 issues)
──────────────────────────────

"I ran a quick technical audit on [domain.com] — using Google's
own PageSpeed tool and a few others. Here's what I found:

  One: [Issue 1, e.g. 'The site doesn't load on mobile devices
  correctly — it fails Google's mobile-friendly test.']

  Two: [Issue 2, e.g. 'The SSL certificate is flagged as untrusted,
  so Chrome shows a warning before visitors even see your site.']

  Three: [Issue 3, e.g. 'The copyright footer still says 2019,
  which signals to Google that the content is outdated.']

These are exactly the things Google looks at to rank a site."

──────────────────────────────
SOFT CLOSE
──────────────────────────────

"I can walk you through the full audit in a 15-minute call —
no cost, no pressure. If at the end you want to handle it
yourself, I'll send you the full report. If you'd like help,
we can talk about options.

Does [specific day, e.g. 'Thursday at 2pm'] work for you?"

  → If YES: book it immediately. Confirm email for calendar invite.
  → If MAYBE: "How about I send you the audit report first so
    you can see exactly what I'm looking at? What's your email?"
  → If NO INTEREST: "Totally fair. If you ever want a second
    opinion on your Google presence, I'm easy to reach.
    Have a great day."

──────────────────────────────
OBJECTION HANDLING
──────────────────────────────

"We already have someone handling our website."
→ "Great — they might not be aware of these specific technical
   signals. I can send you the report so you can share it with
   them and get their take. Would that help?"

"We don't get business from the internet."
→ "That's changing fast in [service type]. [X]% of people now
   Google a service before calling — even if they heard about
   you from a friend. The search is the trust check.
   You might be surprised how much you're leaving on the table."

"How much does this cost?"
→ "The audit is completely free. If you decide you want help
   fixing the issues, we can talk about pricing after you've
   seen the report. There's no obligation at all."

"I don't have time right now."
→ "Completely understood. Can I send you the report by email
   so you can look at it when it suits you?"

WALK-IN SCRIPT (in-person visit)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Best for: restaurants, retail, salons, repair shops, dental/medical
reception desks.

AT THE FRONT DESK:

"Hi — is the owner or manager available? I'm [Name], I'm a local
SEO consultant. I was doing some research on businesses in the area
and found something about [Business Name]'s website I thought the
owner would want to know. It only takes two minutes. Is there a
good time I could come back, or can I leave a note?"

  → Leave a printed one-page audit summary with your card.
  → Follow up by phone 2 days later referencing the visit.

WHAT TO LEAVE:
  • Printed one-page audit comparison (Your site vs. competitor)
  • Business card with phone + email
  • No pitch deck — keep it curiosity-driven
